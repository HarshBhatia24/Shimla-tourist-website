Welcome to Shimla

Discover the rich history and culture of Shimla, the British summer capital.

Museums of Shimla

Himachal State Museum

Offers a deep dive into the history and culture of Himachal Pradesh. The museum features a rich collection of artifacts, including traditional costumes, jewelry, and ancient manuscripts that reflect the diverse cultural heritage of the region.

View on Map

Annandale Army Heritage Museum

This museum displays military artifacts and honors the bravery of Indian soldiers. It showcases a variety of memorabilia, including uniforms, weapons, and photographs that tell the story of the Indian Army's history and its contributions to the nation.

View on Map

Baba Bhalku Railway Museum

Learn about the history of the railway in Shimla. The museum is dedicated to the contributions of Baba Bhalku, a local hero who played a significant role in the construction of the Kalka-Shimla railway. It features vintage locomotives, photographs, and railway memorabilia.

View on Map

Shimla Heritage Museum

This museum provides insights into Shimla's political history and heritage. It showcases artifacts from the British colonial era, including documents, photographs, and personal belongings of prominent figures who shaped the history of Shimla.

View on Map

Image Art Gallery

Showcases a diverse collection of artworks from local and national artists. The gallery hosts exhibitions, workshops, and cultural events, making it a vibrant hub for art lovers and a great place to appreciate contemporary art.

View on Map

Town Hall

Historical building that now serves as a museum. Originally built in the 19th century, it features stunning architecture and houses various exhibitions that highlight Shimla's colonial past and its evolution over the years.

Heritage Sites of Shimla

Viceregal Lodge (Rashtrapati Niwas)

This Gothic-style building was the summer retreat for the British Viceroy. It is surrounded by beautiful gardens and offers guided tours that delve into its architectural significance and the historical events that took place within its walls.

View on Map

Christ Church

One of the oldest churches in North India, known for its stunning stained glass windows. The church is a prominent landmark in Shimla and offers a serene atmosphere for visitors to reflect on its historical and architectural beauty.

View on Map

Jakhoo Temple

Dedicated to Lord Hanuman, this temple is located on Jakhoo Hill, offering panoramic views of the city. It is a popular pilgrimage site and features a giant statue of Hanuman, making it a must-visit for both devotees and tourists.

View on Map

Mall Road

The main street of Shimla, lined with shops, cafes, and colonial architecture. It is a bustling area where visitors can enjoy shopping, dining, and soaking in the vibrant atmosphere of the city.

View on Map

The Governor's House

Also known as the Raj Bhavan, this beautiful colonial building is set amidst lush gardens. It serves as the official residence of the Governor of Himachal Pradesh and offers guided tours that highlight its historical significance and architectural features.

View on Map

Gaiety Theatre

A historic theatre known for its Victorian architecture and cultural performances. It hosts various events, including plays and musical performances, making it a cultural hub in Shimla.
